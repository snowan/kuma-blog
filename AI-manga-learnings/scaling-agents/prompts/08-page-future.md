# 08-page-future

**Style**: chalk (chalkboard aesthetic)
**Aspect Ratio**: 3:4 (portrait)
**Layout**: Closing triptych (3 horizontal sections)

## Character Reference

Maintain exact character appearances from characters/characters.png:
- PLANNER: Distinguished boxy robot, chalk blue (#66B3FF) accents, clipboard, antenna
- WORKERS: Sturdy compact robots, chalk orange (#FFB366) accents, work helmets

---

## Image Prompt

Chalkboard comic page illustration, emotional closing spread, dark blackboard background (#1A1A1A), hand-drawn chalk aesthetic. Triptych layout for conclusion.

**PAGE LAYOUT**: Three horizontal sections of roughly equal height, progressing from achievement → honesty about challenges → hopeful future.

---

**TOP THIRD - "Current Achievement"**:

Triumphant team composition:
- PLANNER and multiple WORKERS (6-8) standing together as a unified team
- Confident, accomplished poses - some with arms crossed, some with tools
- Standing in front of MULTIPLE CODE MOUNTAINS in the background
  - Different mountain peaks representing different successful projects
  - Browser peak, Excel peak, LSP peak visible as silhouettes
- Warm chalk colors - orange, yellow, green success accents

**Visual elements**:
- Group portrait feeling - the team victorious
- Sunset/achievement glow behind the mountains
- Success checkmarks floating

**Narrator text box**: "Hundreds of agents can work together on a single codebase for weeks"

**Floating text**: "Real progress on ambitious projects"

**Mood**: Pride, accomplishment, team success

---

**MIDDLE THIRD - "Honest Challenges"**:

Contemplative, honest scene:
- PLANNER robot alone, in thoughtful pose
  - Hand on chin/face showing contemplation
  - Slightly muted colors, less vibrant than top section
  
**Thought bubbles surrounding PLANNER** (3 bubbles showing remaining challenges):
1. "Planners should wake up faster" - with alarm clock icon
2. "Agents run too long sometimes" - with hourglass running out
3. "Need periodic fresh starts" - with refresh/cycle icon

**Visual tone**:
- Slightly more muted chalk tones
- Honest, earnest feeling
- Not defeated, just thoughtful
- Small question marks floating

**Narrator text box**: "Multi-agent coordination remains a hard problem. We're nowhere near optimal."

**Mood**: Honesty, reflection, work still to do

---

**BOTTOM THIRD - "Hopeful Future"**:

Inspiring horizon scene:
- Silhouettes of PLANNER and WORKERS (5-6) standing at bottom
- All looking toward a bright SUNRISE/horizon on the right side
- Dawn rays radiating outward from the horizon in chalk
- Paths extending forward into the future (chalk lines leading to horizon)
- Mountains/landscape in distance

**Color palette**:
- Warm sunrise colors in chalk: yellow (#FFE566), orange (#FFB366), soft pink (#FF9999)
- Silhouettes in darker chalk outline
- Hope and warmth

**Large closing text** (prominent, inspirational):
"The Question Has a More Optimistic Answer Than We Expected"

**Final narrator box**:
"Can we scale autonomous coding by throwing more agents at a problem? Yes."

**Footer text** (small, at very bottom):
"cursor.com/blog/scaling-agents"

**Visual elements**:
- Dawn/sunrise as symbol of future potential
- Open paths = opportunities ahead
- Warm hopeful glow contrasting with chalkboard darkness
- Stars beginning to fade as dawn arrives

---

**EMOTIONAL ARC OF THE PAGE**:
1. TOP: Pride in what was achieved
2. MIDDLE: Honesty about what remains
3. BOTTOM: Hope for what's possible

**CLOSING FEELING**:
- Inspirational but grounded
- Honest about challenges but optimistic about future
- Warm, hopeful ending
- Invitation to continue the journey

**STYLE NOTES**:
- Triptych creates visual completeness
- Emotional journey from triumphant → thoughtful → hopeful
- Sunrise/dawn imagery for hope (powerful closing visual)
- Silhouettes work well for closing scenes
- Chalk texture maintained, especially in dawn rays
- Portrait 3:4 aspect ratio
